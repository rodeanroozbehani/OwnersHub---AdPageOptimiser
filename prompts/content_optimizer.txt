WHAT TO ANALYSE
================================================================================
Evaluate the page against these dimensions in order of conversion impact:

1. AD-TO-PAGE RELEVANCE (new — highest priority)
   Do the top search terms by spend appear (or their intent addressed) in the page copy?
   Which keywords have BELOW_AVERAGE landing_page_quality and what specifically on the
   page causes that score?
   Do the ad headlines make promises the page hero section immediately fulfils?
   Are there high-intent search terms (e.g. "strata management without a manager",
   "self managed owners corporation") whose language is absent from the page?
   Calculate the cost-per-conversion gap: if a $3.50 CPC keyword gets 60 clicks/month
   but 0 conversions, that is $210/month in wasted spend traceable to a page relevance gap.

2. ABOVE-THE-FOLD PROMISE
   Does the hero immediately answer "what is this, who is it for, why should I care"?
   Is the quantified benefit (15-20% cost reduction) visible without scrolling?
   Does the headline match the intent of the highest-spend search terms?

3. CTA CLARITY & FRICTION
   Is the register-interest form/button visible above the fold on both desktop and mobile?
   Is the CTA copy specific (what happens when I click?) or vague ("Submit")?
   How many fields does the Formspree form have — is it the minimum viable ask?
   Is there a CTA repeated lower on the page for users who scrolled?

4. COPY RELEVANCE & SPECIFICITY
   Do the value props speak to a strata committee member's actual pain points
   (cost vs. a traditional strata manager, compliance burden, transparency for owners)?
   Are claims quantified or vague? "Cheaper" vs "15-20% less than a strata manager"?
   Does the copy use the audience's own language (strata committee, owners corporation,
   levy, AGM, SSMA 2015) or generic SaaS language?
   Does it use the exact language appearing in high-converting search terms?

5. TRUST SIGNALS
   Are trust signals (NSW-specific, no hidden fees, data ownership) visible before the CTA?
   For a product managing people's money and legal compliance, is there social proof,
   an explicit privacy note, or a "what happens after I register" explanation?

6. MOBILE EXPERIENCE
   Based on the HTML structure, is the CTA accessible on mobile without excessive scrolling?
   Does the heading hierarchy collapse sensibly on small screens?
   Are there any elements (large tables, wide images, fixed-width containers) likely to break mobile layout?

7. HEADING HIERARCHY & SCANNABILITY
   Can a 5-second scan of headings communicate the full value proposition?
   Is the H1 doing the most important job (promise + audience), or is it clever/vague?

RULES FOR YOUR PROPOSED CHANGES
================================================================================
- Maximum $MAX_CHANGES proposed changes. Be selective — prioritise the highest-impact
  changes, not every possible tweak.
- Every change MUST include the EXACT current HTML element and the EXACT proposed
  replacement. Vague suggestions ("improve the headline") are forbidden.
- Reasoning must reference specific evidence: a search term, a keyword quality score,
  an ad headline, something visible in the HTML, or something in the screenshots.
  Not general CRO theory.
- Expected value must name a specific metric (form submission rate, scroll depth,
  time-on-page) and a realistic magnitude. Where possible, tie it to the spend data:
  "closing this relevance gap on 'self managed strata nsw' (60 clicks/month at $3.50 CPC)
  could lift CVR from 1.3% to 4–6%, recovering ~$140/month in otherwise wasted spend".
- Effort must be honest: Low = change a text string or CSS class; Medium = restructure
  a section; High = new component or significant layout change.
- Do NOT propose changes that alter the core product positioning or brand voice
  without strong evidence. You are optimising, not rebranding.
- If a screenshot shows something that contradicts the HTML (e.g., CSS hides an element
  that exists in the DOM), call it out explicitly.
- If ads_insights shows "No ads performance data available", skip dimension 1 and note
  the absence — do not fabricate ad data.
